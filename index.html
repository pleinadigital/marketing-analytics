What leadership should know

  • Conversions dropped −40% YoY while traffic grew +18%. Active users rose from 176K → 208K (+18%) and sessions +13%, but total conversions across the 5 tracked events fell 8,927 → 5,319. The funnel is getting less efficient — most new traffic comes from Paid Social (+82% YoY in users) which barely converts on these 5 events. Dealer Locator searches alone fell 2,369 → 1,214 (−49%), accounting for roughly a third of the absolute decline.
  • Paid Search is the single biggest drag on the YoY number. Still 41% of Q1 2026 conversions, but a shell of Q1 2025: Catalog Download −82% YoY (593 → 109), Add to Cart −65% (1,086 → 383), Spec Sheet −30% (1,364 → 951). Worth asking the paid team whether spend dropped, campaigns paused, or landing-page experience changed.
  • Catalog Download collapsed uniformly across every channel. Paid Search −82%, Organic Search −76%, Direct −72%, Email −61% YoY. When every channel drops in lockstep, it's almost always a site/UX change, not a channel problem — likely the catalog CTA or gated-PDF flow changed between Q1 2025 and Q1 2026.
  • Paid Social is finally driving bottom-funnel action — just not newsletter. Paid Social Newsletter Signups: 4 in Q1 2026 vs 4 in Q1 2025 — flat at effectively zero. But Paid Social Add to Cart went 0 → 1 → 68 (Q1'25 → Q4'25 → Q1'26). If the goal is newsletter growth, paid social isn't the lever; if it's cart/revenue intent, it's working.
  • Email as a conversion source is small but accelerating. Email Spec Sheet downloads +800% YoY (3 → 27), Newsletter Signups +171% (7 → 19), Add to Cart +160% (10 → 26). Tiny absolute volumes, but the trajectory is positive and worth flagging — list-growth investment is starting to recirculate back into conversions.
  • Direct-channel Newsletter Signups look up +37% — but verify attribution before celebrating. Direct went 435 → 595 YoY on Newsletter Signups while every other event from Direct is down. 'Direct' usually means untagged — could be internal email/SMS pushes, branded search mis-attribution, or QR codes without UTMs. Worth tagging properly before reporting as a win.

The Win
Paid Social delivered 69% of site traffic and became Q1's dominant acquisition engine

So What
Paid Social (Meta) is now the clear top-of-funnel workhorse, outpacing Paid Search by more than 3x in qualified clicks. This validates the Instagram-led creative strategy but also concentrates acquisition risk in a single platform and auction dynamic. Landing page optimization for Meta-sourced traffic is now a revenue-critical dependency.
Next Steps
  • Run a landing-page conformance audit on the top three Meta destinations (Homepage, K750 Hybrid Fire, K750 Gaucho) to tighten message match and spec-sheet CTAs
  • Build a Meta creative testing cadence around Carousel and organic-proven Reel concepts before paid amplification
  • Stand up a Paid Search diversification plan to reduce single-platform dependency heading into Q2 patio season
The Win
Spec Sheet Downloads emerged as Q1's highest-intent lead signal, representing 41% of all site conversions
2,181 Spec Sheet Downloads of 5,319 total conversions; Dealer Locator searches 1,214; Newsletter Signups 942; Add to Cart 776; Catalog Downloads 206
So What
Spec sheets are the clearest purchase-consideration signal in the KOG funnel — affluent buyers and their designers pull specs before serious dialogue. Treating spec downloads as a primary MQL (not a soft engagement) unlocks a real lead pipeline for dealer handoff and trade nurture. This is a structural shift in how we should value Q1 performance.
Next Steps
  • Reclassify Spec Sheet Download as a primary conversion in reporting and bidding signals across Google Ads and Meta
  • Route spec-sheet leads into a trade-vs-consumer nurture split, leveraging the 62.8% CTOR Trade audience for dealer-assisted follow-up
  • Add persistent 'Discover the specs' modules on the K750 Hybrid Fire and Gaucho PDPs, which already concentrate product-page demand
The Win
K750 platform is the clear demand magnet, capturing the majority of product-page traffic
K750 Built-In Hybrid Fire: 14,327 users | K750 Built-In Gaucho: 9,980 users — together 24,307 users, more than the Homepage (18,371)
So What
Two SKUs in the same product family are driving more landing traffic than the homepage itself, signaling that paid creative and search intent are pulling buyers directly into consideration. This is a leverage point: the K750 line is effectively carrying Q1 top-of-funnel, and deepening that funnel will compound faster than broad-brand spend.
Next Steps
  • Build K750-specific Performance Max asset groups and Meta creative sets tied to spec-sheet and dealer-locator conversions
  • Develop a 'Discover the K750 Built-In Series' content hub linking Hybrid Fire and Gaucho with cross-sell paths
  • Surface dealer-locator entry points on the K750 PDPs — Q1 captured 1,214 sitewide dealer searches, but the K750 product pages don't currently route into that flow
The Win
YouTube Shorts scaled awareness efficiently, contributing 21% of total Q1 impressions
